## FAQ SECTION

**Section header:**
> The questions we know you're already asking.

### Q1: Do I need to know how to code?
**A:** No. Not a line. AgentDoom assembles pre-built, battle-tested components based on what you describe in plain English. The people crushing it on this platform are creators, freelancers, consultants, and operators — not developers. If you've ever had an idea for a tool you wish existed, you're already qualified.

### Q2: How is this different from ChatGPT or other AI builders?
**A:** Those tools give you a generated artifact. A snippet. A component. Something you still have to take somewhere and do something with. AgentDoom gives you a live, deployed, shareable URL in 10 seconds. The output isn't code — it's software. There's a difference.

### Q3: What kinds of tools can I actually build?
**A:** Calculators, trackers, generators, forms, dashboards, quizzes, converters, booking flows, pricing tables — anything you can describe in a sentence. If it can be built from components (and almost everything can), AgentDoom can build it. The 1,800+ tools already deployed in private beta are proof.

### Q4: Is this actually free to use?
**A:** Building and sharing tools is free. Selling tools is where the platform cut applies — we take 15-20% of each transaction. If you want unlimited tools, no branding, custom domains, and analytics, Pro is $14/month. There's no "freemium trap" — free is genuinely useful.

### Q5: What happens to my tools if I stop paying?
**A:** Free-tier tools you've built stay live. You don't lose your work if you don't upgrade. Paid features deactivate — custom domain, no-branding, analytics — but your tools keep running and your marketplace listings stay active. You built it. It's yours.

## COPY NOTES FOR DEV HANDOFF

- Hero CTA should have a subtle glow/pulse animation — not flashy, just alive
- "Claim Your Spot" implies scarcity; pair with real waitlist count that increments
- Feed section should show a live or looping demo of actual tools being generated
- Testimonials: pull from real beta user DMs when available. Prioritize specificity (tool name, dollar amount, use count)
- Stats section: update with real numbers at launch. Never show round numbers — they look fabricated
- Color palette implication: dark background (#0a0a0a or similar), high-contrast white copy, red or violet accent for CTAs
- Typography implication: geometric sans (like Linear or Geist) — no serifs, no playful fonts
- The tone should feel like a manifesto, not a pitch deck
