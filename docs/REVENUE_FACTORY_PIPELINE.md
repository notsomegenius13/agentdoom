# Revenue Factory Pipeline — Unprompted Holdings

## Anti-Pattern Thesis

Anyone with access to agentic models will say "make me a money printer" and build commoditized wrappers. The ideas where the crowd won't follow are ones where domain complexity is high enough that you can't prompt through it in a weekend — you need regulatory frameworks, jurisdiction-specific workflows, supply-side relationships, or proprietary data accumulation.

## The Meta-Pattern
You're not building a product. You're building an orchestration layer between existing supply-side infrastructure and demand that already exists. AI doesn't create demand or replace regulated professionals — it collapses operational overhead from 50-2,000 employees into 1-5 people plus agents.

## Evaluation Criteria (Karpathy Debate Framework)
For every opportunity, Atlas's team must evaluate:
1. **Demand signal strength** — Is there proven willingness to pay? (GLP-1 level demand?)
2. **Barrier profile** — Low enough to enter, high enough to not be commoditized
3. **LTV:CAC ratio** — Must be >10x at scale. >30x is exceptional.
4. **Operational leverage** — Can agents do 90%+ of the work?
5. **Moat type** — Data, switching cost, network effect, regulatory, or geographic?
6. **Time to $10K MRR** — Under 90 days or deprioritize
7. **Compounding dynamic** — Does it get better/cheaper/faster with scale?
